Piggy: The Voice of Reason

An in-depth look at the intellect, tragedy, and symbolism of Piggy in William Golding's Lord of the Flies.

The Glasses

Piggy's spectacles represent science, intellect, and humanity's ability to create fire. As they break, so does the boys' connection to civilization.

The Conch Shell

Though Ralph blows it, Piggy is the true protector of the conch. It symbolizes democracy, order, and the right to speak.

The Tragic Fall

Piggy's ultimate fate at Castle Rock marks the complete death of intellect and reason on the island, leaving only savagery behind.
